Why Follower Count Alone Doesn't Predict Revenue

Free-platform followers and paying subscribers are fundamentally different relationships — someone tapping "follow" costs them nothing, while converting to a paid subscription requires enough trust and perceived value to pull out a card. Realistic free-to-paid conversion rates across creator platforms typically fall between 0.5% and 3%, depending heavily on niche, price point, and how directly you ask people to subscribe.

Engagement rate is a better predictor than follower count. An account with 20,000 highly engaged followers often converts better than one with 200,000 passive followers — this calculator uses a flat rate for simplicity, but your real number depends on how invested your specific audience is.

What Moves Conversion Rate Up or Down

  • Higher: Niche, highly-engaged audiences (finance, hobbyist communities, adult content)
  • Higher: Clear, exclusive value in the paid tier (not just "more of the same")
  • Lower: Broad, general-interest audiences with low per-follower engagement
  • Lower: High price points relative to the platform's typical subscriber
