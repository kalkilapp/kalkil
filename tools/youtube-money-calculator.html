How YouTube Calculates Creator Revenue

YouTube uses two key metrics: CPM (Cost Per Mille — what advertisers pay per 1,000 ad impressions) and RPM (Revenue Per Mille — what you actually receive per 1,000 views). YouTube keeps approximately 45% of ad revenue, so your RPM is roughly 55% of your CPM.

Formula: RPM = CPM × 0.55 × monetization rate — Not every view serves an ad. Your effective monetization rate is typically 70–90%.

Factors That Affect Your YouTube Earnings

  • Audience geography: US/UK/Canada viewers generate 5–10× more revenue than South Asian markets.
  • Video length: Videos over 8 minutes unlock mid-roll ads — the biggest single driver of per-video income.
  • Seasonality: Q4 CPMs are 30–80% higher due to holiday advertiser spend.
  • Ad formats enabled: Non-skippable and bumper ads increase total impressions per view.
