How to Increase Sub Revenue

  • Set visible sub goals on stream — specific milestones motivate viewers to subscribe.
  • Offer compelling sub perks: custom emotes, ad-free viewing, Discord roles, badge progression.
  • Host sub-a-thon events — dedicated multi-hour streams focused on hitting subscriber milestones.
  • Encourage Tier 2 and Tier 3 subs — a single Tier 3 earns 5× a Tier 1.
  • Leverage gifted subs — reward top gifters with special recognition to encourage more gifting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Twitch takes 50% compared to YouTube's 45% or Patreon's 5–12%. Amazon (which owns Twitch) has faced creator criticism for not improving the split. Legacy 70/30 deals existed but were phased out for new partners in 2023.
Prime subs earn the same creator revenue (~$2.50 at the 50% split) even though they cost the viewer nothing. Amazon subsidizes the creator payment from Prime subscription revenue.
The vast majority of monetized Twitch streamers have under 100 active subscribers. A streamer with 200 subs is in the top 10–15% of monetized creators on the platform.

Final Thoughts

Twitch subscriptions are the most reliable income stream on the platform. At the 50% split, it takes discipline to build meaningful sub count — but the community that subscribes is also the community that donates bits, watches ads, and participates in sponsorships. Every sub is a compounding asset.
