How Much Does YouTube Pay Per 1,000 Views?

CPM, RPM, and real earnings data for every niche — so you know exactly what your views are worth.

Home› Guides› How Much Does YouTube Pay Per 1,000 Views?

The short answer: YouTube pays between $3 and $6 per 1,000 views on average through its RPM (Revenue Per Mille) model. But that average hides enormous variation — a finance channel might earn $15 per 1,000 views while a gaming channel earns $2.

Key distinction: CPM is what advertisers pay YouTube. RPM is what you, the creator, actually receive — roughly 55% of CPM after YouTube's revenue share.

How YouTube Calculates Creator Revenue

YouTube splits ad revenue with creators at roughly a 45/55 ratio — you keep 55%. Your RPM is calculated as:

RPM = (Total Estimated Revenue ÷ Total Views) × 1,000

Not every view earns revenue — only views where an ad was successfully served count toward your RPM. Ad blockers, skipped pre-rolls, and non-monetized content all reduce your effective rate.

Factors That Affect Your YouTube Earnings

  • Audience geography: US/UK/Canada viewers generate 5–10× more revenue than South Asian markets.
  • Niche: Finance and legal niches attract premium advertisers.
  • Video length: Videos over 8 minutes can include mid-roll ads — dramatically increasing total impressions.
  • Seasonality: Q4 (Oct–Dec) CPMs are 30–80% higher than Q1 due to holiday ad spend.
  • Engagement: High watch time keeps viewers through more ad breaks.
