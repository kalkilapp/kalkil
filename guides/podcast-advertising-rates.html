Podcast Advertising Rates Explained

What brands pay to advertise on podcasts — and how to price your show's inventory.

Podcast advertising is one of the highest-ROI channels in digital marketing, with consistently strong purchase intent among listeners. Here's what brands pay and how to price your show's ad inventory in 2026.

Pricing Your Podcast Sponsorships

CPM method: Rate = (Monthly Downloads ÷ 1,000) × CPM

Example: 25,000 monthly downloads × $30 mid-roll CPM = $750 per episode

Flat rate for smaller shows:

Monthly DownloadsFlat Monthly Rate

50,000+$3,000–$30,000

Sponsorship Deal Types

  • Single episode: Lower commitment, standard rates.
  • Series (4–10 episodes): Brands pay a premium for continuity — 10–20% above single-episode rates.
  • Exclusive category: Brand gets category exclusivity in your show — commands 2–3× standard rates.
  • Baked-in reads: Host-read, permanent in the episode — higher CPM and listener trust than dynamically inserted ads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most ad networks require 1,000+ downloads per episode. However, direct sponsor outreach can work at any size — even a 500-listener niche show on business software can attract relevant B2B sponsors.
Baked-in reads (permanent in the episode) earn higher CPMs and feel more authentic to listeners. Dynamic insertion scales more efficiently for larger catalogs but feels less personal.
Start with direct outreach to brands in your niche. Podcast advertising marketplaces (AdvertiseCast, Spotify Audience Network, Podchaser) connect shows to brands. At scale, talent agencies manage sponsorship relationships.

Final Thoughts

Podcast advertising rewards niche authority over raw download counts. A finance show with 10,000 downloads earns more per episode than a general comedy show with 50,000 — because the audience is worth more to advertisers. Price accordingly.
