How Streaming Revenue Is Calculated

From royalty pools to your bank account — the complete mechanics of streaming income.

Home› Guides› How Streaming Revenue Is Calculated

Many artists receive streaming payments without understanding the calculation behind them. The process involves multiple steps, several parties, and two distinct royalty types. Understanding it helps you verify you're receiving everything you're owed.

The Pro-Rata Calculation Model

  1. 1Platform collects revenue: Subscriptions + advertising for the month.
  2. 2Royalty pool established: ~70% (Spotify) or ~73% (Apple Music) allocated to rights holders.
  3. 3Your stream share calculated: Your streams ÷ all platform streams = your % of the pool.
  4. 4Deductions applied: Label, distributor, and publisher cuts reduce the gross amount.
  5. 5Net payment issued: Your distributor or label pays you on their schedule.

Two Types of Streaming Royalties

Royalty TypeWho Receives ItCollected Via
Master (Sound Recording)Sound recording owner (artist or label)Distributor → Artist
Mechanical / PublishingSongwriter and publisherPRO + Music Publisher

Independent artists who write their own music are owed both royalty types. Failing to register with a PRO and publisher means leaving 10–20% of your streaming income uncollected.

Pro-Rata vs. User-Centric Models

Pro-rata (current standard): All streams count equally. Mega-artists dominate the pool. Indie artists with small but loyal fanbases earn less per fan engagement.

User-centric (emerging): Each subscriber's fees go to the artists they personally listened to. Rewards loyalty over volume. Tidal and SoundCloud have experimented with this model — and it would benefit most independent artists.

Frequently Asked Questions

Each platform has different subscription pricing, advertiser revenue, subscriber counts, and royalty pool allocation percentages — all of which combine to produce different effective per-stream rates.
Register your songs with a PRO (ASCAP, BMI, PRS), sign up with a music publisher or publishing administrator, and use a reputable distributor who provides transparent reporting.
A royalty pool is the total amount set aside from platform revenue for distribution to rights holders. It grows as subscriber counts and platform revenue grow, but so does the denominator (total streams), keeping per-stream rates relatively stable.
