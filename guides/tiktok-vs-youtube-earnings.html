TikTok vs YouTube Earnings

Two very different paths to creator income — which one is right for you?

Home› Guides› TikTok vs YouTube Earnings

TikTok and YouTube are both dominant creator platforms — but they monetize completely differently. YouTube rewards long-form content with premium ad rates. TikTok rewards viral reach and brand deal potential. Choosing the right one depends on your content type and income strategy.

Ad Revenue vs. Creator Rewards

YouTube AdSense pays per video via a direct CPM model — creators keep 55% of ad revenue from their specific content. High-niche content earns $15–$40+ RPM.

TikTok Creator Rewards pays from a shared pool based on platform-wide engagement. Maximum effective RPM rarely exceeds $0.08 per 1,000 views.

YouTube pays 75–150× more per view in direct platform revenue than TikTok. But TikTok gets you to brand deals faster.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose TikTok if you want rapid audience growth, create short entertaining content, and prioritize brand deals over passive ad income.

Choose YouTube if you create educational, how-to, or review content and want per-view revenue that compounds passively over years.

Best strategy: both. Use TikTok for growth and brand deals. Use YouTube for long-form authority content and AdSense income. They compound each other.
